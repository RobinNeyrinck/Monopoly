Beerpoly®

The Wolfpack types of
beer Trading Game

In 2022, The Wolpack of Vlaanderen, Belgium, presented a game called BEERPOLY to the executives of The Wolfpack.The Wolfpack was unemployed at the time and often played this game to amuse themselves and pass the time. It was the game’s exciting promise of fame and fortune that initially prompted The Wolfpack to produce this game on their own. With help from lectors who are good helpers, The Wolfpack made much fame of the BEERPOLY game. As the demand for the game grew, The Wolfpack could not keep up with the players. Since 2022, when The Wolfpack acquired the rights to the game, it has become the leading proprietary game not only in Belgium but throughout the Western World. As of 2022, the game is published under license in 43 countries

Objective

The object of the game is to become the wealthiest player through buying, renting and selling property.

Equipment

The equipment consists of a timeline, 2 dice, tokens, 32 breweries and 12 factories. There are Chance and Community Chest cards, a Title Deed card for each property and play money.

Preperation

Open the game online. You decide with how much players you want to play(2-4). Each player chooses one coloured pawn to represent him/her while traveling around the timeline. Each player is given $1500 divided as follows: 2 each of $500’s, $100’s and $50’s; 6 $20’s; 5 each of $10’s, $5’s and $1’s. All remaining money and other equipment go to the Bank.

Banker

The banker and the auctioneer is the computer itself

The bank

Besides the Bank’s money, the Bank holds the Title Deed cards and breweries and factories prior to purchase and use by the players. The Bank pays salaries and bonuses. It sells and auctions properties and hands out their proper Title Deed cards; it sells breweries and factories to the players and loans money when required on mortgages. The Bank collects all taxes, fines, loans and interest, and the price of all properties which it sells and auctions. The Bank never “goes broke.” If the Bank runs out of money, the Banker may issue as much more as may be needed by writing on any ordinary paper.

The play

Starting with the Banker, each player in turn throws the dice. The player with the highest total starts the play: Place your pawn on the corner marked “GO,” throw the dice and move your token in the direction left to right the number of spaces indicated by the dice. After you have completed your play, the turn passes to the left. The pawns remain on the spaces occupied and proceed from that point on the player’s next turn. Two or more pawns may rest on the same space at the same time. According to the space your pawn reaches, you may be entitled to buy types of beer or other properties — or obliged to pay rent, get non-alcoholic beer, draw a Chance or Community Chest card, “Go to the EHBO ® ,” etc. If you throw doubles, you move your token as usual, the sum of the two dice, and are subject to any privileges or penalties pertaining to the space on which you land. Retaining the dice, throw again and move your pawn as before. If you throw doubles three times in succession, move your pawn immediately to the space marked “In the EHBO”(see EHBO).

Go

Each time a player’s pawn lands on or passes over GO, whether by throwing the dice or drawing a card, the Banker pays him/her a $200 salary. The $200 is paid only once each time around the board. However, if a player passing GO on the throw of the dice lands 2 spaces beyond it on Community Chest, or 7 spaces beyond it on Chance, and draws the “Advance to GO” card, he/she collects $200 for passing GO the first time and another $200 for reaching it the second time by instructions on the card.

Buying a beer type

Whenever you land on an unowned type of beer you may buy that type of beer from the Bank at its printed price. You receive the Title Deed card showing ownership. You can see it in status. If you do not wish to buy the type of beer, the Banker sells it at auction to the highest bidder. The buyer pays the Bank the amount of the bid in cash and receives the Title Deed card for that type of beer. Any player, including the one who declined the option to buy it at the printed price, may bid. Bidding may start at any price.

Paying rent

When you land on a type of beer owned by another player, the owner collects rent from you in accordance with the list printed on its Title Deed card. If the type of beer is mortgaged, no rent can be collected. When a type of beer is mortgaged, its Title Deed card is placed face down in front of the owner. It is an advantage to hold all the Title Deed cards in a color-group (e.g., Cara and Leffe) because the owner may then charge double rent for unimproved type of beers in that color-group. This rule applies to unmortgaged type of beers even if another type of beer in that color-group is mortgaged. It is even more advantageous to have breweries or factories on type of beers because rents are much higher than for unimproved type of beers. The owner may not collect the rent if he/she fails to ask for it before the second player following throws the dice.

"Chance" & "community chest"

When you land on either of these spaces, you will get a random card from the computer, follow the instructions and return the card to the computer. The “Get Out of EHBO” card is held until used and then returned to the computer. If the player who draws it does not wish to use it, he/she may sell it, at any time, to another player at a price agreeable to both.

Income TAX

If you land here you have two options: You may estimate your tax at $200 and pay the Bank, or you may pay 10% of your total worth to the Bank. Your total worth is all your cash on hand, printed prices of mortgaged and unmortgaged type of beers and cost price of all buildings you own. You must decide which option you will take before you add up your total worth.

EHBO

You land in the EHBO when… (1) your pawn lands on the space marked “Go to EHBO”; (2) you draw a card marked “Go to EHBO”; or (3) you throw doubles three times in succession. When you are sent to EHBO you cannot collect your $200 salary in that move since, regardless of where your pawn is on the board, you must move it directly into EHBO. Yours turn ends when you are sent to EHBO. If you are not “sent” to EHBO but in the ordinary course of play land on that space, you are “Just looking at your drunk friend” you incur no penalty, and you move ahead in the usual manner on your next turn. You get out of EHBO by… (1) throwing doubles on any of your next three turns; if you succeed in doing this you immediately move forward the number of spaces shown by your doubles throw; even though you had thrown doubles, you do not take another turn; (2) using the “Get Out of EHBO Free” card if you have it; (3) purchasing the “Get Out of EHBO Free” card from another player and playing it; (4) paying a fine of $50 before you roll the dice on either of your next two turns. If you do not throw doubles by your third turn, you must pay the $50 fine. You then get out of EHBO and immediately move forward the number of spaces shown by your throw. Even though you are in the EHBO, you may buy and sell type of beers, buy and sell breweries and factories and collect rents.

Happy hour

A player landing on this place does not receive any money, type of beers or reward of any kind. This is just a “HAPPY” resting place.

Breweries

When you own all the type of beers in a color-group you may buy breweries from the Bank and erect them on those type of beers. If you buy one brewery, you may put it on any one of those type of beers. The next brewery you buy must be erected on one of the unimproved type of beers of this or any other complete color-group you may own. The price you must pay the Bank for each brewery is shown on your Title Deed card for the type of beer on which you erect the brewery. The owner still collects double rent from an opponent who lands on the unimproved type of beers of his/her complete color-group. Following the above rules, you may buy and erect at any time as many breweries as your judgement and financial standing will allow. But you must build evenly, i.e., you cannot erect more than one brewery on any one type of beer of any color-group until you have built one brewery on every type of beer of that group. You may then begin on the second row of breweries, and so on, up to a limit of four breweries to a type of beer. For example, you cannot build three breweries on one type of beer if you have only one brewery on another type of beer of that group. As you build evenly, you must also break down evenly if you sell breweries back to the Bank (see SELLING PROPERTY).

Factories

When a player has four breweries on each type of beer of a complete color-group, he/she may buy a factory from the Bank and erect it on any type of beer of the color-group. He/she returns the four breweries from that type of beer to the Bank and pays the price for the factory as shown on the Title Deed card. Only one factory may be erected on any one type of beer.

Building Shortages

When the Bank has no breweries to sell, players wishing to build must wait for some player to return or sell his/her breweries to the Bank before building. If there are a limited number of breweries and factories available and two or more players wish to buy more than the Bank has, the breweries or factories must be sold at auction to the highest bidder.

Selling type of beers

Unimproved type of beers, railroads and utilities (but not buildings) may be sold to any player as a private transaction for any amount the owner can get; however, no type of beer can be sold to another player if buildings are standing on any type of beers of that color-group. Any buildings so located must be sold back to the Bank before the owner can sell any type of beer of that color-group. Breweries and factories may be sold back to the Bank at any time for one-half the price paid for them. All breweries on one color-group must be sold one by one, evenly, in reverse of the manner in which they were erected. All factories on one color-group may be sold at once, or they may be sold one brewery at a time (one factory equals five houses), evenly, in reverse of the manner in which they were erected.

Mortages

Unimproved type of beers can be mortgaged through the Bank at any time. Before an improved type of beer can be mortgaged, all the buildings on all the type of beers of its color-group must be sold back to the Bank at half price. The mortgage value is printed on each Title Deed card. No rent can be collected on mortgaged type of beers or utilities, but rent can be collected on unmortgaged type of beers in the same group. In order to lift the mortgage, the owner must pay the Bank the amount of the mortgage plus 10% interest. When all the type of beers of a color-group are no longer mortgaged, the owner may begin to buy back breweries at full price. The player who mortgages type of beer retains possession of it and no other player may secure it by lifting the mortgage from the Bank. However, the owner may sell this mortgaged type of beer to another player at any agreed price. If you are the new owner, you may lift the mortgage at once if you wish by paying off the mortgage plus 10% interest to the Bank. If the mortgage is not lifted at once, you must pay the Bank 10% interest when you buy the type of beer and if you lift the mortgage later you must pay the Bank an additional 10% interest as well as the amount of the mortgage.

Bankruptcy

You are declared bankrupt if you owe more than you can pay either to another player or to the Bank. If your debt is to another player, you must turn over to that player all that you have of value and retire from the game. In making this settlement, if you own breweries or factories, you must return these to the Bank in exchange for money to the extent of one-half the amount paid for them; this cash is given to the creditor. If you have mortgaged type of beer you also turn this type of beer over to your creditor but the new owner must at once pay the Bank the amount of interest on the loan, which is 10% of the value of the type of beer. The new owner who does this may then, at his/her option, pay the principal or hold the type of beer until some later turn, then lift the mortgage. If he/she holds type of beer in this way until a later turn, he/she must pay the interest again upon lifting the mortgage. Should you owe the Bank, instead of another player, more than you can pay (because of taxes or penalties) even by selling off buildings and mortgaging type of beers, you must turn over all assets to the Bank. In this case, the Bank immediately sells by auction all type of beers so taken, except buildings. A bankrupt player must immediately retire from the game. The last player left in the game wins.

Miscellaneous

Money can be loaned to a player only by the Bank and then only by mortgaging type of beers. No player may borrow from or lend money to another player.

RULES for a SHORT GAME (60 to 90 minutes)

There are five changed rules for this first Short Game. 1. During PREPARATION, the Banker shuffles then deals three Title Deed cards to each player. These are free— no payment to the Bank is required. 2. You need only three breweries (instead of four) on each lot of a complete color-group before you may buy a factory. Factory rent remains the same. The turn-in value is still one-half the purchase price, which in this game is one brewery less than in the regular game. 3. If you land in EHBO you must exit on your next turn by 1) using a “Get Out of EHBO Free” card if you have (or can buy) one; or 2) rolling doubles; or 3) paying $50. Unlike the standard rules, you may try to roll doubles and, failing to do so, pay the $50 on the same turn. 4. The penalty for landing on “Income Tax” is a flat $200. 5. END OF GAME: The game ends when one player goes bankrupt. The remaining players value their type of beers: (1) cash on hand; (2) lots, utilities and railroads owned, at the price printed on the board; (3) any mortgaged type of beer owned, at one-half the price printed on the board; (4) breweries, valued at purchase price; (5) factories, valued at purchase price including the value of the three breweries turned in. The richest player wins!

ANOTHER GOOD SHORT GAME

TIME LIMIT GAME…

Before starting, agree upon a definite hour of termination, when the richest player will be declared the winner. Before starting, the Banker shuffles and cuts the Title Deed cards and deals two to each player. Players immediately pay the Bank the price of the type of beers dealt to them.
